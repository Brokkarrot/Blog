Time to get Baking!

With the fall weather comes the fall fruits! A personal favorite of mine is pumpkin. Pumpkins are a versatile fruit which can be cooked, blended, juiced, carved, and baked. Here, I'd like to share with you this recipe that is a staple for the fall weather. Here is what you'll need:

  • Pumpkin- 2 Cups (Canned or Fresh)
  • Yeast- 1 Packet
  • Water- 1/4 Cup
  • Flour- 4 1/2 Cups
  • Salt- 1/2 Tsp.
  • Sugar- 1/2 Cup
  • Butter 4 Tbs.
  • 1 Egg
  • Baking Safe String
  • Walnuts (Optional)
As You all know, I'm not a baker. I just like to experiment. Just as a heads up, if your bread doesn't come out that well, you can always improvise. That's what I always do so good luck. Now lets get started: Add the yeast to the 1/4 Cup of water. Wait 15 minutes or until the yeast if frothy. Mix all the dry ingredients together. Mix the pumpkin with the egg and butter. Once the yeast mixture is set, combine with pumpkin mix. Combine everything! Knead the dough for 10 minutes then leave to rest for 30 minutes, covered by a towel or plastic wrap. When ready, punch down dough and divide into equal sized pieces. Wrap baking string around dough making 6 bumps to form the pumpkin shape. Add the walnuts on tops. Let those rest for 10 minutes under towel or plastic. Preheat oven for 375 degrees Farenheit. Bake the buns for 20-25 minutes. Once done, let cool and enjoy.
